FAQ: Frequently Asked Questions

What exactly is the Odor Genome Protocol?
The Protocol is a 30-point biological diagnostic. We analyze your dermal sensitivity, diet, lifestyle, and sweat output to precisely calculate your unique biochemical needs.
Why do most people fail when they switch to natural deodorant?
The failure is a chemistry mismatch. If you have a high load of odor-causing bacteria but choose a formula that is primarily absorbent without strong anti-microbial agents, it will fail.
Is Baking Soda (Sodium Bicarbonate) actually a problem?
Baking soda is a powerful odor neutralizer, but it's very alkaline (high pH). If your skin's natural acidic mantle is sensitive, it can cause severe rashes or burns. Our protocol identifies this risk immediately.
What is the "Detox Period"?
When you quit aluminum, your body purges toxins. Your bacteria also shift rapidly, often leading to temporary, stronger odors for 2-4 weeks.

The Odor Lexicon

Apocrine Sweat

The oily, protein-rich sweat produced by stress and hormones. This is the primary fuel source for odor-causing bacteria.

Bioconversion

The chemical process where bacteria (like *Corynebacterium*) digest odorless sweat compounds and convert them into pungent, volatile acids.

AHA/BHA (Targeted Acids)

Alpha/Beta Hydroxy Acids (e.g., Mandelic Acid). Used to lower the armpit's pH, creating an acidic barrier that inhibits bacterial growth.

Zinc Ricinoleate

A powerful chemical compound derived from castor oil. It traps and neutralizes odor molecules.
