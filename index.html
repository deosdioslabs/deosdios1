FAQ: Frequently Asked Questions

What exactly is the Odor Genome Protocol?
The Protocol is a 30-point biological diagnostic. We analyze your dermal sensitivity, diet, lifestyle, and sweat output to precisely calculate your unique biochemical needs. We then cross-reference this data against a curated database of high-performance deodorants to find your perfect match.
Why do most people fail when they switch to natural deodorant?
The failure is a chemistry mismatch. If you have a high load of odor-causing bacteria but choose a formula that is primarily absorbent (like arrowroot) without strong anti-microbial agents, it will fail. Our job is to match your microbial environment to the right active ingredient.
Is Baking Soda (Sodium Bicarbonate) actually a problem?
Baking soda is a powerful odor neutralizer, but it's very alkaline (high pH). If your skin's natural acidic mantle is sensitive, it can cause severe rashes or burns. Our protocol identifies this risk immediately.
What is the "Detox Period"?
When you quit aluminum, your body purges toxins. Your bacteria also shift rapidly, often leading to temporary, stronger odors for 2-4 weeks. We recommend using charcoal masks to accelerate this period.

The Odor Lexicon

Apocrine Sweat

Stress-induced, protein-rich secretions that act as primary fuel for odor-causing bacteria.

Bioconversion

The process where skin bacteria convert odorless sweat into pungent, volatile organic compounds.

AHA/BHA (Targeted Acids)

Alpha/Beta Hydroxy Acids (e.g., Mandelic Acid). Used to lower the armpit's pH, creating an acidic barrier that inhibits bacterial growth.

Zinc Ricinoleate

A powerful chemical compound derived from castor oil. It traps and neutralizes odor molecules.
