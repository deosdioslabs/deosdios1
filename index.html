Frequently Asked Questions

What is the Odor Genome Protocol?
It is a 30-point diagnostic engine. We intake data on your dermal sensitivity, dietary sulfur output, apocrine vs. eccrine sweat triggers, and lifestyle factors to generate a biochemical match score against 75+ products.
Why does Baking Soda cause rashes?
Baking Soda (Sodium Bicarbonate) has a pH of about 9 (Alkaline). Human skin has a pH of 5.5 (Acidic). For many, this gap causes the "acid mantle" to break down, resulting in a red, peeling chemical burn often mistaken for an allergy.
What is the "Detox Period"?
When you stop using aluminum antiperspirants, your sweat glands unplug and your microbiome shifts. This often results in 2-4 weeks of increased moisture and pungency as your body re-equilibrates. We recommend using a charcoal mask to speed this up.

The Odor Lexicon

APOCRINE SWEAT

Thick, protein-rich sweat produced by stress. The main food source for bacteria.

ECCRINE SWEAT

Watery, cooling sweat. Generally odorless until mixed with bacteria.

CORYNEBACTERIUM

The specific bacteria genus responsible for "pungent" or "sour" body odor.

MANDELIC ACID

An Alpha Hydroxy Acid (AHA) that kills odor-causing bacteria by lowering skin pH.
